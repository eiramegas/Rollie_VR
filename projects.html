Rollie VR Projects

Rollie VR consists of 2 parts, the cardboard headset that facilitates the VR experience, and the VR experience itself

About the Headset

Two of the largest limitations of traditional virtual reality technology are the size/setup and the incredible cost. At Rollie VR, we have adapted the Google Cardboard lenses into a wearable, fun, and fashionable helmet that costs even less and is easier and quicker to assemble.

About the VR Experience

As the goal of Rollie VR is aid in the development of empathy for those with various diseases and disorders, we have created a comprehensive, interactive and educational virtual tour for a number topics (click here for a list of projects or to suggest one!). Each environment includes a menu of rooms that explore the disease or disorder in a different way:

  • Science behind the disorder
  • Case study documenting real-life experiences living with the disorder
  • An example of an assistive technology for the difficulties that come with the disorder
  • More about Rollie VR and its other applications

You can immerse yourself into these different worlds just by loading the VR environment onto your smartphone using the Google Cardboard app and viewing it through our VR helmet!

Emanuel Syndrome Project

Olivia's senior design project at Marquette was a communication device for an eight year old girl with Emanuel Syndrome, which is a chromosomal disorder that disrupts normal development and different parts of the body (More Info Here). Our Project creates a virtual reality experience to

  1. Explain the scientific background of Emanuel Syndrome
  2. to make a realistic and interactive space to understand what it is like to experience the different symptoms of the syndrome.
Then, a solution to one of the symptoms, inability to communicate, will be present to introduce the idea that biomedical engineers can develop solutions to everyday difficulties like the ones people with Emanuel Syndrome experience.

Details of Emanuel Syndrome

Typically, people have 46 chromosomes: 2 pairs of each chromosome 1-22. Individuals with Emanuel Syndrome have one extra chromosome, a combination of chromosome 11 and 22, which is called derivative 22 or der(22) shown below. This extra chromosome causes the delay in intellectual development delay and body anomalies.

The idea for this would be to go down the helix of the chromosomes and highlight 11 and 22. Then, the VR would show them melding together to become der(22).
